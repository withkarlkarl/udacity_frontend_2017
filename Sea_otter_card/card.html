Sea Otter

Sea otter is a marine mammal native to the coasts of the northern and eastern North Pacific Ocean

  • Body Feature: Adult weigh between 14 and 45 kg
  • Unique: exceptionally thick coat of fur
  • Food: It preys mostly on marine invertebrates
  • Special: It can use of rocks to open shells

Sea otters, classified as an endangered species, were hunted extensively for their fur between 1741 and 1911, and the world population fell to 1,000–2,000 individuals from 150,000–300,000. A subsequent international ban on hunting, conservation efforts, and reintroduction programs into previously populated areas have contributed to numbers rebounding, and the species now occupies about two-thirds of its former range
